Sentiment analysis of Danish texts (wordlist)

The process of determining the emotional tone behind a series of words, used to gain an understanding of the the attitudes, opinions and emotions

Sentiment analysis — also known as opinion mining — is as simple as assigning each word in a text a value based on the bias of the word, after which the overall sentiment is decided by summarizing the values.
This is especially useful for larger companies, who wish to gain an insight in their customers opionions regarding a certain topic. However, it could also be used as a tool for students analysing a Danish text, as it can help to quickly locate all positively or negatively charged words.

In this post we will be analysing a Danish text with a wordlist-based approach using AFINN; although this so called wordlist-based approach has its flaws, as it fails to identify eg. sarcasm. It is a very naive approach and is therefore better suited for text consiting of simple sentences or formal language. For more complex sentences a better choice would involve training a model, which calculates the polarity by forming a relationship between the words. However that said, it is still an execellent tool and extremely useful in certain scenarios.

We will be using the afinn package, as it supports the Danish language, although when it comes to English, textblob is the preffered choice.
